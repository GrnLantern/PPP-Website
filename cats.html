Cat Care and Diet

The second most popular domestic pet in the United States, cats were originally domesticated about 9500 years ago in Western Asia. Breeds come with their own unique set of guidlines for care and diet but with over 60 different cat breeds in the world it can be a lot to catalogue. Here we will provide general care and diet information for cats of any breed, broken down into age categories. Please alway check with you Vet about diet plans specific to your cat breed.

0 to 4 Weeks Old

Whenever possible it is best practise to have kittens of this age nurse directly from their mother. Kittens should be monitered closely to ensure they are growing at the correct weight and are healthy. Kittens may need to be fed from a bottle or other feeding device if it is noted that they are not gaining weight. Kittens may not gain weight at an appropriate for several different reasons including but not limited to:

  • too much competiting from litter mates over mothers milk
  • digestive issues or disease
  • unhealthy conditions or environment conditions
Formula should be specifically designed for kittens and should be served at body temperature. The Spruce Pets has an article on best kitten formulas that can be found here.

4 Weeks to 1 Year Old

Around 3 to 4 weeks after birth kittens should be introduced to solid food. Initially this should be done by mixing formula with wet kitten food. The Cat Nutrition Page of the ASPCA website suggests kittens should eat between hald and a cup of dry food or six to 9 ounces of wet food per day. It also states that kittens struggling to eat dry food can have small amounts of water added to the food to make it softer. Ensure you are using food for kittens as they provide the correct balance of nutrients to help your kitten grow. Architechtural Digest has a list of "The Best Kitten Food of 2024" here.

1 to 7 Years Old

No longer considered kittens, cats in this age range need less nutrients. Cats within this age range should be fed twice a day and food should NOT be left out between feedings. After 1 year old cats level of activity will decrease and their metabloism will start to slow, by ensuring your cat is only eating at the appropriate times and the correct amounts you can help reduce the risk of obesity and other health issues. Cats.com has a list of their top picks for cat foods here.

Over 7 Years Old

Like all living beings our elderly felines need different care and activity levels than their younger counterparts. Cats at this stage should be fed a diet of higher protiens and lower fats and calories. It is important to read product labels for specific protein types, like salmon, as oppossed to broader categories like "meat" which could contain filler products and less proteins.
